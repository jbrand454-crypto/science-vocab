This is the brief on velocity. Picture this. You have two cars going the exact same speed, but they're heading in completely different directions. Right away, you can see why speed alone doesn't really give us the full picture. First, let's lock in the definition. Velocity equals speed plus direction. It's that simple, but it reminds us that the which way is just as crucial as the how fast. Second, we need to bust a major misconception because speed and velocity are definitely not the same thing. I mean, if I tell you I'm running 10 miles an hour, do you know where I'll end up? No way. Speed is simply a number, but velocity demands a destination. Third, let's look at a quick analogy. Imagine giving someone direction to a party. It would be totally absurd to just tell them, drive fast. For them to actually arrive, you have to say, "Drive fast" toward downtown. To summarize in one line, direction is what gives speed its true meaning. And well, this beautifully bridges science and faith. We see this exact principle reflected in Hebrews 12-1, which calls us to run with perseverance the race marked out for us. A busy life without direction toward God is exactly like speed without velocity. It is just motion without meaning. Is your life just busy? Or is it actually headed somewhere?
