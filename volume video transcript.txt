Look at this giant fluffy pillow sitting next to this tiny metal dumbbell, which one of these takes up more space, and which one is heavier? Drop them on a scale, and the tiny dumbbell slams the needle down, while the mass of pillow barely registers. But wait, only measures their mass. Let's look at the actual physical space they take up. The pillow fills a vastly larger amount of this three-dimensional measuring grid. This exact measurement of occupied physical space is called volume, and volume doesn't just measure solid boundaries. We can use it to measure internal capacity, too. Think of volume as how much water it takes to completely fill an empty container, like a small cup versus a large jug. Because the larger jug has more volume, it inherently requires far more water to completely fill its base. So while that tiny metal dumbbell is much harder to lift, the giant fluffy pillow will always have way more volume.
