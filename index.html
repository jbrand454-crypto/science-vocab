Field Notes: How This Works

For parents, staff, and anyone curious what this is

What This Is

A free, no-login science vocabulary review tool for grades 6–8, cross-referenced with national and state science standards. Students pick a grade, then work through this week's new terms or older review terms, at their own pace, on any device — phone, tablet, or school Chromebook.

What This Isn't

Field Notes itself never records a grade, a score, or a name anywhere — it stores nothing about your student, and the site has no login. Most of it is purely for practice and review. The one exception: each weekly new term includes a "Reflect (Graded)" section with a link to a separate, official weekly journal entry — a short written reflection that does count toward a grade, submitted through a Google Form and recorded in the gradebook like any other assignment. Everything above that section on a term's page stays practice-only.

How To Navigate

  • Grade tabs (6 / 7 / 8 / All Grades) — pick a grade level to start.
  • 🆕 New — this week's 3 new vocabulary terms.
  • 🔁 Review — 3 older terms brought back for spaced repetition.
  • 📚 Practice All — every term taught so far this year (and, for 7th/8th grade, every term from earlier years too, since those grades are cumulatively responsible for prior content).
  • Each term card has a plain-English definition, an example, a common misconception, an analogy, a self-check question, and a Faith & Science in Action reflection — plus buttons to simplify, get another example, take a quiz, or compare to a related term.
  • "Explore More" holds an optional short video, infographic, or podcast per term, where available.

✝️ A Note on Faith & Science

Every term includes a short Faith & Science in Action reflection, connecting that science concept to Scripture and our Catholic tradition. These reflections aren't meant to be a finished answer — they're meant to open a conversation. We encourage parents and staff to sit with your student for a minute and talk through it together: ask what the connection means to them, whether they see it differently, or where else they've noticed God's hand in the natural world. Science and faith aren't in competition here — both are ways of encountering the same Creator, and some of the richest learning happens in that honest, unhurried conversation, not just in reading a screen alone.

📝 Weekly Reflection & Grading

Select terms include a "Reflect (Graded)" section — this is the one part of Field Notes that counts toward a grade. Students write a short response covering three things: the science fact, a real example, and an honest answer to that term's Faith & Science in Action question. It's assigned early in the week and due by the end of the week, so there's no rush.

Scoring is simple by design — students write one combined paragraph covering the science fact, an example, and the Faith & Science connection together. It's scored on two things: Content (0 to 3 points) and Grammar & Structure (0 to 2 points), for 5 points possible, combined into one gradebook entry per week. The goal is thoughtful, genuine writing, not length or polish.

  • 2 — Clean writing, complete sentences, no distracting errors
  • 1 — Understandable, but has noticeable errors
  • 0 — Errors get in the way of understanding it

A bonus point is available for completing the reflection before the end of the school day on the second-to-last day of the school week — an incentive to work ahead rather than wait until the last minute, which also means most grading is already done well before the week ends. And a Content score of 1 (as opposed to 0, 2, or 3) isn't a final word — it's a signal that a response showed real effort but stayed shallow, and that student may be asked to revise with more depth before a final score is recorded.

🎧 Listen or Watch Anywhere

Every term's video and podcast are also being collected into a full library, so students and parents can listen or watch through the whole set like a regular show — in the car, on a walk, wherever's easiest.

Standards Alignment

Every term is tagged with a Disciplinary Core Idea code, part of the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) — the national framework most states' science standards, including Ohio's, are built on. Click "📋 See the Standard" on any term to read the actual end-of-unit mastery goal it supports, straight from that framework. Because these codes are standards-based rather than tied to any single textbook, this tool works alongside whatever curriculum a school already uses.

Why This Matters Beyond This Week

Vocabulary is the foundation science reasoning is built on — a student can't apply a concept they don't yet have language for. Strong term fluency supports how students perform across the standardized science assessments used throughout the year, even though this tool itself is review, not a test-prep program.

It also carries further than this year. The Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) build their core ideas as a K–12 progression (kindergarten through 12th grade), not separate silos per grade. The same standard codes tagged on every term here (like PS1.A or LS2.B) continue directly into high school science — a student who walks in already fluent in "atom," "element," or "force" is extending a foundation, not starting from zero.

And because this page is always free, requires no login, and works identically on any device at any hour, it removes the biggest practical barrier to consistent review — the students who benefit most are often the ones without a tutor, a quiet study space, or reliable access at a fixed time.

Updated Weekly

The "New" and "Review" terms rotate every week to match what's being taught in class, so this stays a living companion to instruction rather than a static list.
