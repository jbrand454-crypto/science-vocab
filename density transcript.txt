How do we measure the amount of matter stuffed inside an object? That's density. Density is how tightly matter is packed into a specific space. The formula is simple. Mass divided by volume. To see this, compare a same-sized foam block and metal block. The metal is denser because far more matter is packed into that identical space. This is why oil floats on water. Oil is just less dense than water. And remember, a larger object is not automatically denser than a small one. So two objects are the same size, but one has more mass, which one is denser?
