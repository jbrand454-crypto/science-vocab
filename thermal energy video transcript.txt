Which of these everyday objects has more total thermal energy? You'd probably guess the tiny cup of boiling water, right? Because it's much hotter, its tiny water particles are moving and jiggling around incredibly fast. But thermal energy isn't just a measure of temperature or speed. It is the total sum of energy from every single moving particle inside an object combined. So while the boiling cup has a few superfast particles bouncing around, the giant bathtub is packed with a massive army of slower moving dots. Because the tub contains vastly more matter, all those billions of extra jiggling particles start to add up. This means the warm bathtub actually holds a much higher total amount of thermal energy. So while a tiny piece of glowing coal burns incredibly hot and would instantly burn your hand, a massive freezing coal lake holds vastly more total thermal energy, simply because of its sheer volume of moving particles.
