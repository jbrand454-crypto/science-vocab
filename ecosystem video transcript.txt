When you look at a pond ecosystem, do you just see the animals? Or is there more to it? Let's break down this typical backyard pond. You've got fish swimming, frogs resting, and insects buzzing. These are the obvious living parts. It's a busy community sharing the exact same space. But watch what happens if we isolate just the animals. Without a solid physical foundation, the living community immediately begins to struggle. That's because an ecosystem also requires the non-living surroundings. We need the water they swim in, the sunlight powering the plants, and the resting soil at the bottom. Notice how they physically support the living organisms. The entire space works together like a unified team. So a complete ecosystem requires every single piece of this puzzle. It only survives because those living frogs and fish are deeply connected to the non-living water and sunlight around them.
