Look at these two cars. Both are moving at exactly 40 miles per hour. Are they doing the exact same thing? Same speed, but are they really the same? That 40 miles per hour is just their speed. It only measures how fast they're going, but leaves out their exact direction. To get the full picture, you combine speed with exact direction, a measurement called velocity. Because one car heads north and the other heads south, their velocities are completely different, even though their speeds are identical. What happens if you lock your cruise control at 40, but take a sharp turn? Your speed stays the same, but the second your directional arrow shifts, your velocity changes entirely. So the next time you look down at the dashboard while driving, remember your speedometer is only telling you half the story. You need to know where you're pointed to know your true velocity.
