When we think about force, we usually expect things to move. So if someone pushes against a solid wall with everything they have, and nothing moves, are they still applying a force? Now contrast that with a tiny gentle nudge on a small ball. The massive physical struggle produces absolutely zero motion, while the tiny tap creates obvious movement. It feels like motion is what makes a force real, but in physics, a force is strictly the push or the pull itself. It is the physical action, completely separate from the acceleration that might happen as a result. So when you gently tap that ball, you are applying a small force vector, and that intense struggle against the unmoving wall from the beginning, even without a single inch of visible motion, that person is still applying a massive undeniable force.
